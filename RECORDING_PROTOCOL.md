# 🎤 Clinical Voice Recording Protocol for Parkinson's Detection

## 🎯 Primary Recording Task: Sustained Phonation

### What to Record:
1. **"Ahhhh" sound (/a/)** - 3 recordings, 3-15 seconds each
2. **"Ohhh" sound (/o/)** - 3 recordings, 3-15 seconds each  
3. **"Ehhh" sound (/e/)** - 3 recordings, 3-15 seconds each

**Total: 9 recordings minimum**

## ⚙️ Technical Requirements

- **Format:** WAV (uncompressed, NOT MP3/M4A)
- **Sample Rate:** 44.1 kHz or higher
- **Bit Depth:** 16-bit minimum (32-bit preferred)
- **Microphone Distance:** 15-20 cm from mouth
- **Environment:** Quiet room (<40 dB background noise)

## 📝 Step-by-Step Instructions

### Preparation:
1. Sit upright in comfortable position
2. Use a good quality microphone or smartphone
3. Find a quiet room with minimal echo
4. Remove jewelry that might create noise
5. Clear throat gently before starting

### Recording Process:
1. **Take a deep breath**
2. **Say the vowel sound as steadily as possible**
3. **Maintain consistent volume** (comfortable speaking level)
4. **Keep pitch as steady as possible**
5. **Continue until you run out of breath naturally**
6. **Don't force or strain your voice**

### Between Recordings:
- Rest 10-15 seconds between each recording
- Breathe normally
- Swallow if needed to clear throat

## 🎬 Recording Sequence

### Vowel 1: "Ahhhh" (/a/)
1. Recording 1: Take breath → "Ahhhh" (steady, 3-15 seconds)
2. Rest 15 seconds
3. Recording 2: Take breath → "Ahhhh" (steady, 3-15 seconds)  
4. Rest 15 seconds
5. Recording 3: Take breath → "Ahhhh" (steady, 3-15 seconds)

### Vowel 2: "Ohhh" (/o/)
Repeat same process with "Ohhh" sound

### Vowel 3: "Ehhh" (/e/)  
Repeat same process with "Ehhh" sound

## 🔄 Additional Tasks (Optional but Helpful)

### Rapid Speech Tasks:
- Say "pa-pa-pa" as fast as possible for 10 seconds
- Say "ta-ta-ta" as fast as possible for 10 seconds
- Say "ka-ka-ka" as fast as possible for 10 seconds

### Reading Task:
Count from 1 to 20 at normal speaking pace

### Spontaneous Speech:
Describe your morning routine for 1-2 minutes

## ✅ Quality Checklist

Before submitting recordings, ensure:
- [ ] No background noise or interruptions
- [ ] Consistent volume throughout recording
- [ ] Clear, steady vowel sound
- [ ] No voice breaks or cracks
- [ ] WAV format (not compressed)
- [ ] At least 3 seconds duration per recording
- [ ] Proper microphone distance maintained

## 💡 Why These Specific Sounds?

- **Sustained vowels** reveal voice stability issues
- **Jitter and shimmer** are measurable in steady sounds
- **/a/ vowel** is most commonly used in Parkinson's research
- **Multiple repetitions** ensure measurement reliability
- **Uncompressed audio** preserves acoustic details needed for analysis

## ⚠️ Important Notes

### Medical Disclaimer:
- This is for research/screening purposes only
- Not a substitute for professional medical diagnosis
- Consult a neurologist if you have motor symptoms
- Voice changes can have many causes besides Parkinson's

### Research Basis:
- Based on Parkinson's Voice Initiative protocols
- Used in clinical studies worldwide  
- Validated for detecting voice changes in Parkinson's
- Standard protocol in speech pathology research

## 🎯 Key Differences from Casual Recording

| Clinical Protocol | Casual Recording |
|-------------------|------------------|
| Sustained vowel sounds | Normal speech |
| WAV uncompressed | MP3/M4A compressed |
| Specific duration (3-15s) | Variable length |
| Multiple repetitions | Single recording |
| Controlled environment | Any environment |
| Consistent microphone distance | Variable distance |

**This is why your casual M4A recording showed different results!**
